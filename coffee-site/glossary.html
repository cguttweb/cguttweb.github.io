Coffee Terms

  • Light Roast - a light roast has toasted grain taste more of the original flavours retained most of the caffeine is retained
  • Medium Roast - a medium roast has more body than the light roasts with a more balanced flavour, aroma and acidity contains less caffeine than the light
  • Dark Roast - a dark roast has a bitter/smoky taste with the original flavours eclipsed by the roasting process it contains less caffeine.
  • Single Origin - coffee grown within a single known geographic region/origin. However as there are no real rules so single origin could mean:
    • coffee completely from one farm
    • coffee from multiple farms in the same country
    • coffee from multiple farms in the same general area
  • Blended coffee - this phrase usually refers to a mix of coffee beans containing different roasts, styles or coffee tastes. Purists discussing blends generally mean the actual bean mixture giving rise to the brew
  • Speciality coffee - a term coined in 1978 by Erna Kutsen it is a simple concept that special geographic micro climates produce beans with unique flavour profiles
  • Espresso - a short shot of coffeee the base to all your favourite coffee-shop drinks
  • Single serve - a pod based coffee machine e.g. Nespresso or Tassimo
